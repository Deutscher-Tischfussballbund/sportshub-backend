Admin app · process map

Who does what, in what order

Four roles run the season end to end — a region admin who owns the competition, a club admin who owns the club's teams, a captain who owns one roster, and a global admin who oversees all of it. A region admin can also delegate one league's day-to-day running to a league admin, who gets that same region-admin authority but narrowed to a single league. This maps every step each of them takes, in the order they actually happen, as built in the admin app today.

Global admin— sees every region
Region admin— runs one federation's competitions
League admin— runs one league, appointed per league
Club admin— runs one club's teams
Team admin / captain— runs one team's roster
01

Set up the season

The region admin opens each season and decides when teams may register. Most seasons don't start from a blank page — copy-forward seeds the whole league tree and every placement from a prior season in one step, rosters included if most teams are staying put.

02

Stand up a team, then register it

A team is a standing identity, separate from any one season — the club admin creates it once and hands the captain the keys. From there the captain registers for any open league in the region themselves, or the region admin places the team directly.

03

Manage placements through the season

One table, one region admin (or that league's own league admin, if one's been appointed), four moves. Removing a team only works before it's played anything — once a match day or a standing exists for it, the only way out is to withdraw it, which keeps that history intact instead of erasing it.

Why withdraw exists Match and Standing rows point at the team directly, not at the placement. Deleting a placement with history wouldn't even fail — it would just orphan that history from the record of where the team was placed. Withdrawing keeps the row instead of removing it, so the season's story stays intact. What withdrawing does Flips the placement to WITHDRAWN. Its roster can no longer be edited, and it's skipped the next time this season gets copied forward.
04

Run the roster

The roster has its own small lifecycle, separate from the placement it belongs to. A captain drafts and submits it; someone above the team — a region/club admin, or that league's league admin, but never the person who submitted it — signs off. Any of them can also step in and edit a roster at any time, registration window or not.

Where confirmation happens The region's Confirm rosters view lists every SUBMITTED roster in that region, waiting on a sign-off — but that queue is region/club-admin only. A league admin doesn't see it; they confirm by opening their league's roster directly from the Placements table instead. Roster size If the league's rule set sets a minimum or maximum, it's enforced here — a captain can't submit under the minimum, and can't add past the maximum.

Not in the admin app yet

  • League / tier / group structure. Creating or editing these directly (outside copy-forward) is API-only today — there's no admin-app page for it yet.
  • Match day results. Submitting and confirming a fixture's score is built and authorized on the backend (a participating team's admin submits, the opponent confirms), but has no admin-app page yet.
  • Self-service member registration. A public "claim your DTFB-ID account" flow is designed but parked — there's no backend endpoint for it yet.
